What is GI Synergy?

GI Synergy is three bottles from Apex Energetics that are meant to be run as a team during the antimicrobial part of a SIBO protocol. The star is K-64, a botanical blend built around berberine, artemisinin (from sweet wormwood), oregano, goldenseal, and magnolia bark. Why a blend instead of one herb? Coverage. Berberine takes on the gram-negative bacteria and a good share of the methane-producing microbes, oregano handles the gram-positives, and artemisinin and magnolia push into the biofilms that let an overgrowth shrug off a single herb. The second bottle, K-22, is a binder made from zeolite, charcoal, and apple pectin, and its whole purpose is to catch the toxins released as bacteria die so your body does not simply soak them back up. The third, K-29, leans on broccoli seed extract (a sulforaphane source) and milk thistle to support your liver through the work. Herbal antimicrobials have a respectable track record in SIBO — a well-known Johns Hopkins study put herbal protocols roughly on par with rifaximin for easing symptoms — though that speaks to the approach more than to this one product. Your label has the specifics.
